Achievements

Global Recognition in Education Innovation

Strathmore University's Macheo Achievement Programme, which supports students from informal settlements, won the *Gold Award* in the Blended and Presence Learning category at the 2022 Reimagine Education Awards—often referred to as the "Oscars of Education." This accolade recognized the university's innovative approach to combining physical and digital learning to expand access to quality education.

Leadership in Entrepreneurship and Innovation

Through initiatives like iLabAfrica and iBizAfrica, Strathmore has become a hub for technological research and entrepreneurship. @iLabAfrica focuses on ICT research aligned with the UN Sustainable Development Goals, while @iBizAfrica has supported over 400 startups, fostering innovation within Kenya's tech ecosystem.

Prestigious Academic Rankings

Strathmore Business School (SBS) has consistently ranked among the top business schools in Africa. Notably, it was ranked 1st in Kenya and 8th in Africa by Webometrics in 2014

Pioneering Green Campus Initiatives

SBS is recognized as Africa's first green business school. Its campus was awarded for being the Best Green Building Development in Africa in 2012, reflecting the university's commitment to sustainability

Promoting Academic Excellence

Strathmore hosts annual mathematics contests, offering scholarships to top-performing students. In 2024, the university awarded full undergraduate scholarships to outstanding participants, reinforcing its dedication to nurturing academic talent

Ongoing Projects

Kenya Small Business Development Centers (SBDCs)

Strathmore University Business School is spearheading the establishment of SBDCs across Kenya, a project funded by USAID. These centers aim to support Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) by providing access to business development services, training, and resources. The initiative seeks to stimulate economic growth, create employment opportunities, and enhance the competitiveness of Kenyan businesses in both local and international markets

Circular Economy Powered Renewable Energy Centre (CEPREC)

In a significant stride towards sustainable energy solutions, Strathmore University has joined forces with a consortium of African and UK institutions to launch CEPREC. This £3.5 million initiative focuses on developing scalable microgrids that repurpose end-of-life electronic materials, addressing both energy access challenges and electronic waste management in sub-Saharan Africa. Strathmore's role involves technical innovation and behavioral research to ensure the solutions are both effective and culturally appropriate

AI-Powered Forest Protection Initiative

Strathmore University's School of Computing and Engineering Sciences is leading a groundbreaking project aimed at combating illegal logging in Kenyan community forests. In collaboration with international partners, the team is developing an AI-driven system that utilizes sound-based detection techniques to identify and report unauthorized logging activities in real-time. This initiative not only aids in preserving vital forest ecosystems but also contributes to climate change mitigation efforts by maintaining carbon stocks
